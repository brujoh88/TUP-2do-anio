## Section 1.1 - Reading activities

### Exercise 1.1.2 - Sequencing words (p.4)

**Consigna:** Completar el texto "The steps in website development" con las palabras: After that, Finally, First, Next, Secondly, Then, Thirdly.

| # | Respuesta | Oracion |
|---|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **First** | discuss with the customer their requirements and the target audience. |
| 2 | **Secondly** | analyse the information from the customer. |
| 3 | **Thirdly** | create a website specification. |
| 4 | **Next** | design and develop the website. |
| 5 | **After that** | assign a specialist to write the website content. |
| 6 | **Then** | give the project to programmers for HTML coding. |
| 7 | **Finally** | test the website. |

**Explicacion:** Son *sequencing words* (palabras de secuencia) para ordenar pasos en un proceso. El patron natural es: ordinales primero (First, Secondly, Thirdly), luego conectores de continuidad (Next, After that, Then), y Finally para cerrar.

**Consigna:** Ordenar los pasos para crear una pagina HTML basica usando sequencing words.

| Orden | Sequencing word | Paso |
|-------|----------------|------|
| 1 | **First** | C. Open your code editor |
| 2 | **Next** | B. Include the basic structure: `<html>`, `<head>`, `<body>` |
| 3 | **Then** | A. Add a main title using `<h1>` |
| 4 | **After that** | E. Write a short paragraph with `<p>` |
| 5 | **Finally** | D. Save the file as **index.html** |

**Explicacion:** El orden sigue la logica real de trabajo: primero abris el editor, luego armas la estructura base del HTML, despues agregas contenido (titulo, parrafo), y por ultimo guardas el archivo.

### Exercise 1.2.4 - Before reading: typical websites (p.5)

**Consigna:** Pensar en un sitio web tipico y responder las preguntas. Son preguntas abiertas de opinion para activar vocabulario antes de leer.

**a. Which web pages do most websites have?**
*(¿Que paginas tiene la mayoria de los sitios web?)*

> Most websites have a **Home page**, an **About Us** page, a **Contact** page, a **Products or Services** page, and a **FAQ** page.

**b. Which items are often at the top of each page? Which are below that?**
*(¿Que elementos suelen estar arriba? ¿Cuales debajo?)*

> At the top of a web page, there is usually a **logo**, a **navigation bar** and a **search bar**. Below that, there is often a **banner** or main content area.

**c. What makes a website easy or difficult to use? What makes it interesting?**
*(¿Que hace que un sitio sea facil o dificil de usar? ¿Que lo hace interesante?)*

> A website is easy to use when it has **clear navigation** and information is **easy to find**. It is interesting when it has **good images**, **videos**, and an **attractive design**.

### Exercise 1.2.8 - Reading comprehension: "A web developer's work" (p.6-7)

**Consigna:** Leer el texto sobre Andrea y responder las preguntas.

**1. What is Andrea's job?**
> She is a **web developer**.

**2. What is Andrea doing now?**
> She is **adding new features** to make the website better.

**3. What project did Andrea finish recently?**
> She worked on a **website for SLFan**, a fan club for the Space Liberator game.

**4. Where is the logo on the website?**
> The logo is **in the center, at the top** of the page.

**5. Where is the search bar?**
> The search bar is **on the right and below the banner**.

**6. What is below the banner?**
> The **search bar** is below the banner.

**7. Where are the contact details?**
> The contact details are **at the bottom** of the page.

**8. What tools did Andrea and her team use for the website?**
> They used **PHP, HTML5, and MySQL**.

**9. What did Andrea do to make the site easy to use on mobile phones?**
> She **tested the site on mobile phones and tablets**. She checked every page and made sure it looked good.

**10. What do the customers say about the project?**
> The customers say that they are **happy with the results**. They liked the work.

**Tip para responder preguntas de comprension:** Casi siempre podes "reciclar" palabras de la pregunta y completar con la parte relevante del texto. No necesitas inventar, solo ubicar la info y armar la oracion. Ejemplos:
- Pregunta: "What is...?" → Respuesta: "She/It is..."
- Pregunta: "What is ... doing?" (presente continuo) → Respuesta: "She is + verbo-ing..."
- Pregunta: "Where is...?" → Respuesta: "It is + preposicion de lugar..."
- Pregunta: "What did...?" (pasado) → Respuesta: "She/They + verbo en pasado..."

### Exercise 1.2.9 - Categorize phrases: easy, difficult, interesting (p.7)

**Consigna:** Clasificar las frases segun: ¿que hace a un sitio web facil de usar, dificil de usar, o interesante?

**Easy to use** *(facil de usar — todo lo que facilita la navegacion y el acceso):*
- Clear and simple navigation *(navegacion clara y simple)*
- Well-organized menus *(menus bien organizados)*
- Responsive design - works on mobile devices *(diseño responsivo - funciona en celulares)*
- Fast loading speed *(velocidad de carga rapida)*

**Difficult to use** *(dificil de usar — todo lo que obstaculiza o frustra):*
- Too many ads *(demasiados anuncios)*
- Broken links *(links rotos)*
- Poor readability - bad colors or fonts *(mala legibilidad - malos colores o fuentes)*

**Interesting** *(interesante — todo lo que atrae visualmente o engancha con contenido):*
- Attractive visual design - colors, layout *(diseño visual atractivo - colores, disposicion)*
- Relevant and updated content *(contenido relevante y actualizado)*
- High-quality photos *(fotos de alta calidad)*
- Interactive elements - videos, animations *(elementos interactivos - videos, animaciones)*

**Tip para categorizar:** Preguntate: ¿esto ayuda a NAVEGAR? → Easy. ¿Esto MOLESTA o IMPIDE usar el sitio? → Difficult. ¿Esto ATRAE o ENGANCHA al usuario? → Interesting.
